We make EHR data accesible and useful

Electronic Health Records are a one-way street – clinicians put data in, but they can not retrieve any information from these records.

“The current EHR's are not designed to ensure the data can be stored unambiguously, and as a result extracted..."

Translated from “Registratie aan de bron” The Netherlands Federation of University Medical Centres (NFU April 2013)

Searching in electronic health records remains a major hurdle. Since most valuable patient information is archived as text (clinical notes, transfer notes, multidisciplinary meeting notes, discharge letters, etc.) into the Electronic Health Records (EHR), it is not possible to automatically search these criteria. Manual search through unstructured patient records is very time consuming. Based on the latest innovations in information retrieval, natural language processing and machine learning, CTcue enables clinicians to use valuable data from their EHR with user-friendly interfaces.

Compliance

Our application is developed based on “privacy by design”. Before entering patient data in our search engine, We pseudonymize all data including documents, decursus, reports, et cetera. So our application offers a search on premise on non-identifiable data, which allows different hospital employees to browse the EHR data. Today many employees work with the EHR to help search for patients manually, which violates the privacy act (Dutch Wbp).

EY’s HvG specialized privacy attorneys validated our process, especially with respect to the 2016 legislation about the Bill on Notification of data leaks (“meldplicht data lekken”).
